# Chiang Mai Food Research
Compiled 2026-07-18 for Wanderlane. Sources: Michelin Guide Thailand, Tripadvisor, Eating Thai Food, The Infatuation, Time Out Chiang Mai, Chiang Mai Citylife, Wanderlog, official school sites, and cross-referenced blogs. Exchange rate used: **≈33.5 THB = US$1** (July 2026 spot rate).

**Confidence key**
- **High** = confirmed across 3+ independent sources, consistent name/location/details, recent (2026) activity signals
- **Medium** = confirmed but thinner sourcing, or some conflicting details (spelling, exact price)
- **Low** = mentioned in guides but couldn't fully pin down (address, current status) — verify before publishing as a hard recommendation

| **Huen Phen** (เฮือนเพ็ญ) | Old City, Ratchamanka Rd, just south of Wat Chedi Luang | Dishes from ~50 THB (~$1.50) at the lunch counter | 62+ years running; the best single stop to pair khao soi with sai ua, tam khanun (young jackfruit salad), and a huge spread of Northern dishes in one sitting | Two different modes: cheap/casual lunch (8:30am–4:30pm) vs. pricier, more formal dinner service (5–10pm, less khao-soi-focused, more Thai-Burmese); reviews are mixed (~3.6/5) — some find it milder than the stalls | High |

**The top khao soi verdict:** for a first-timer with one shot, **Khao Soi Mae Sai** — Michelin Bib Gourmand, best beef-to-broth ratio, open normal tourist hours, no confusing schedule gotchas. **Khao Soi Khun Yai** and **Lung Prakit Kad Kom** are the "for the story" picks (bring cash, check the day of the week first).

## 2. Must-try Northern Thai dishes beyond khao soi

| Dish | What it is | Where to try it |
|---|---|---|
| **Sai Ua** (ไส้อั่ว) | Coarse-ground pork sausage packed with lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaf, turmeric, and dried chili, grilled over charcoal | Warorot/Ton Lamyai market stalls; Ton Payom Market (Sai Ua La Wan stall); Huen Phen; any night market grill stand |
| **Nam Prik Ong** (น้ำพริกอ่อง) | A tomato-and-chili dip cooked with minced pork — sometimes called "Thai bolognese" — eaten with sticky rice, raw/steamed vegetables, and pork crackling | Huen Phen; Ton Payom Market; sit-down Lanna restaurants (rarely found street-side) |
| **Nam Prik Noom** (น้ำพริกหนุ่ม) | Milder, smokier green chili dip of roasted green chilies, shallots, and garlic, pounded to a coarse paste | Warorot Market; Ton Payom Market; served alongside sai ua almost everywhere |
| **Gaeng Hang Lay** (แกงฮังเล) | Burmese-influenced pork belly curry simmered with tamarind, ginger, and pickled garlic — no coconut milk, deeply savory-sour | Aroon Rai; Huen Phen; Ginger Farm Kitchen |
| **Khanom Jeen Nam Ngiao** (ขนมจีนน้ำเงี้ยว) | Fresh rice vermicelli in a spicy-sour tomato and pork-blood broth, thickened with dok ngiao (cotton-tree flower) and fermented soybean (thua nao) | Khao Soi Lam Duan Fa Ham (its "sister dish"); Nam Ngiew Loong Pong ("Uncle Pong," 40 years, Sriphoom); Warorot Market stalls |
| **Larb Moo Kua / Northern Larb** | Dry-fried minced pork with toasted rice powder and a dark, complex spice mix — very different from Isaan-style larb | Hong Tauw Inn; most Northern Thai restaurants on the menu |

| Market | Days & hours | What to eat | Vibe / notes | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sunday Walking Street** (Ratchadamnoen Rd) | **Sundays only**, road closes ~4pm, stalls fully up by 5–6pm, winds down ~10–11pm/midnight | Khao soi, sai ua, som tam, mango sticky rice, khanom krok (coconut pancakes), grilled skewers | The big one — runs the length of Ratchadamnoen through the Old City (Tha Phae Gate to Wat Phra Singh); temple courtyards (Wat Phra Singh, Wat Chedi Luang) become food courts with tables under trees; half crafts/half food, elbow-to-elbow after 7pm — go early to browse, late to eat | High |

| **Warorot Market** (Kad Luang) | Indoor market daily ~5/6am–6/7pm; outdoor night-market alley alongside it 5pm–11pm; **open every day of the year except major Thai holidays (e.g., Songkran)** | Sai ua, moo yo sausage, nam prik noom, khanom jeen, plus packaged curry pastes/dried herbs/coffee to take home | The real working market — where locals shop, not a tourist show. | **Ploen Ruedee Night Market** | Nightly, roughly 5pm–midnight | Curated mix of Thai and international food stalls, craft drinks, live bands | Smaller, cleaner, more organized "food court bazaar" feel near the Night Bazaar/Charoen Prathet Rd — eco-friendly (no foam containers) but pricier and more tourist-aimed than Warorot or Chang Phueak; best on weekdays to dodge crowds | High |

## 6. Practical tips

**Cash culture**
- Thailand is still cash-first outside hotels/malls: street food, markets, and most khao soi/local restaurants above are cash only or cash-preferred.
- Notes run ฿20/50/100/500/1,000. Breaking a ฿1,000 note at a small stall is often difficult — keep a stock of ฿20s, ฿50s, and ฿100s for street food and markets.
- ATM withdrawal fees have risen in 2026 (roughly ฿250–350 per foreign-card withdrawal) — withdraw larger amounts less often, or use a fee-free travel card where accepted.

**Spice levels**
- Asking for "mai pet" (ไม่เผ็ด, "not spicy") often gets a dish with zero chili — technically safe but flavor-flattened. "Phet nit noy" (เผ็ดนิดหน่อย, "a little spicy") usually keeps the dish's intended balance while dialing down the heat.
- Thai "not spicy" still runs hotter than most Western palates expect. On day one, consider ordering the same dish twice — once "mai pet," once "phet nit noy" — to calibrate before ordering the rest of the trip.

**When markets are closed / schedule gotchas**
- Sunday Walking Street = Sundays only. Saturday Walking Street = Saturdays only. Don't show up on the wrong night.
- Warorot Market is open daily year-round except major Thai holidays (e.g., Songkran in April).
- Several legendary khao soi shops keep unusual hours: Khao Soi Khun Yai is closed Sundays and often sells out by early afternoon; Khao Soi Lung Prakit Kad Kom is closed weekends entirely (Mon–Fri only); Khao Soi Lam Duan Fa Ham and Khao Soi Samer Jai are lunch/early-afternoon only with no dinner service. Always have a backup khao soi spot in mind.

**Hygiene / where locals eat**
- The single best safety signal is turnover: busy stalls with a line of Thai locals, food cooked fresh and served hot, and visibly clean/organized prep areas.
- City ice in Chiang Mai (factory-made, cylindrical with a hole) is generally safe; be more cautious with ice in rural areas outside the city.
- Skip unlabeled communal sauce bottles on the table (refilled indefinitely); use packaged condiments or fresh lime/chili instead.

**Tipping**
- Not expected at street stalls or casual local eateries. At mid-range sit-down restaurants, rounding up or leaving ฿20–40 is appreciated. At upscale restaurants without a service charge, ~10% is appropriate. Thai massage: ฿50–100 for a standard session.
