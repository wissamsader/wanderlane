3 Days in Berlin: The Perfect Itinerary

A day-by-day plan that covers the monuments, the Wall, and the Sonnenallee food crawl most Berlin itineraries miss entirely.

Three days is enough to cover Berlin's essential monuments, its Cold War history, and — because almost no other itinerary sends you here — an afternoon on Sonnenallee, the Levantine food street that's become one of the city's defining food scenes. Here's how to sequence it.

Day 1: Historic Mitte

Start at the Brandenburg Gate, free and open whenever you arrive. From there it's a short walk to the Reichstag glass dome — assuming you booked a slot before you landed (see our things-to-do guide for why that matters), the 360° spiral walk is worth building your morning around. The Holocaust Memorial sits right between the two, free and always open — give it twenty honest minutes. Spend the afternoon on Museum Island, and end the day at the TV Tower for sunset over Alexanderplatz.

☼ Book before you land, not on the day

The Reichstag dome is the one reservation on this whole itinerary that can actually fall through — some 2026 dates close it to visitors entirely. Lock in a slot as soon as you have travel dates.

Day 2: The Wall, the Cold War, and Kreuzberg

Walk the East Side Gallery in the morning — 1.3km of the Berlin Wall repainted by more than a hundred artists, quieter before midday. From there, head to Checkpoint Charlie; on its own it's five minutes, so pair it with a guided Cold War or Third Reich walking tour, consistently one of Berlin's best-selling bookings for a reason. Spend the rest of the afternoon and evening in Kreuzberg — Berlin's counterculture heart, with legendary street food and a strong craft-beer and late-bar scene to close out the day.

Day 3: choose your ending

Your third day is the one place we'd actually let you pick your own adventure — both options below are a full day on their own, so don't try to combine them.

Option A: Museum Island, properly this time

If Day 1 only gave you time for one or two of the five Museum Island museums, come back and do it right: the Neues, the Altes, the Alte Nationalgalerie and the Bode are all worth a slower look than a single afternoon allows. Just confirm what's actually open in the Pergamon before you build the day around it — see our things-to-do guide for the current caveat.

Option B: The Sonnenallee food crawl + nightlife

Take the S-Bahn or U7 into Neukölln for the afternoon and work through our Sonnenallee food crawl — Syrian shawarma, Yemeni cooking, Palestinian falafel and Damascene sweets, all within a few blocks of each other. From there, Friedrichshain and Kreuzberg's club scene is a short ride away if you want to end the trip properly; just know that Berghain's door policy is entirely its own thing, and no amount of planning gets you past it.

Where to base yourself for these three days

Mitte puts Days 1 and 2 within walking distance and is the easiest default — see our full stays guide for specific hotels. If Day 3's food crawl is the real priority, basing yourself in Neukölln or Kreuzberg instead turns that afternoon into a ten-minute walk rather than a special trip across town.

Practical notes for the three days

  • From the airport: BER sits about 18km out; the Airport Express (FEX) reaches Hauptbahnhof in roughly 22 minutes, running every 15 minutes, so you can land and start Day 1 the same afternoon.
  • Getting around: the BVG network (U-Bahn, S-Bahn, trams, buses) covers all three days easily — Mitte, Kreuzberg and Neukölln are also genuinely walkable between stops.
  • The Berlin WelcomeCard bundles unlimited transit with museum and attraction discounts across multi-day options — price it against single tickets for your exact plan before buying.
  • Best timing: May through September gives you the mildest weather; aim for May, September or early October for that same mild window with thinner crowds.

Frequently asked questions

Is 3 days enough for Berlin?

Yes, for the essentials — Brandenburg Gate, the Reichstag, Museum Island, the Wall and one full afternoon on Sonnenallee. Give it four or five days if you want to add a club night, a slower museum pace, or both Day 3 options instead of just one.

Which day should I do the Sonnenallee food crawl?

Day 3 works best in this itinerary, after the core monuments and Cold War history are done — but it's flexible. If food is your priority over a second museum day, swap it in earlier without disrupting Days 1 or 2.

What should I book before I land in Berlin?

The Reichstag dome, first — it has real availability risk and some dates close entirely. A Museum Island pass and a Cold War walking tour slot are worth booking a few days ahead too, though they're less likely to sell out completely.
