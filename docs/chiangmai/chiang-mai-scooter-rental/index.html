Real rental shops we checked on the ground, honest monthly prices, and how to rent without getting stung on deposits or insurance.

A scooter is how Chiang Mai opens up — the hill temples, the cafés down random sois, the waterfalls. Here are rental shops we actually verified (open, operating, prices from their own listings as of mid-2026), plus how to rent smart.

☼ Quick orientation

Expect roughly ฿1,500–2,500 a month for a 125cc automatic (that's about €40–66), or ฿200–300 a day short-term. A ฿1,000 cash deposit is normal — never leave your passport as deposit; leave a copy or a cash deposit instead.

How to rent without regrets

  1. Inspect and video the bike at handover — walk around filming every scratch before you ride off, so a pre-existing dent isn't billed to you later.
  2. Check the brakes, lights, horn and tyres on the spot, and that the seat storage locks.
  3. Always wear the helmet. Police checkpoints are common and fine helmet-less riders; more importantly, it's your head.
  4. Leave a cash deposit, not your passport. A reputable shop is fine with cash or a passport copy.
  5. Get an International Driving Permit before you travel. Legally you need one (with the motorcycle category) to ride here, and travel insurance won't pay a medical claim if you were riding illegally or without a helmet.
⚠ The insurance catch nobody mentions

Most travel-insurance motorbike cover — including SafetyWing's — only applies to engines up to 50cc, but nearly every scooter you'll rent here is 110–125cc. Read your policy's motorbike clause before you ride, carry the right licence/IDP, and never ride without a helmet, or a claim can be denied. Ride within your ability; the mountain roads are not the place to learn.

Frequently asked questions

How much does it cost to rent a scooter in Chiang Mai?

Roughly ฿1,500–2,500 per month (about €40–66) for a 125cc automatic, or ฿200–300 per day short-term, usually with a ฿1,000 refundable deposit. Monthly-specialist shops that deliver to your door are often the cheapest.

Do I need a licence to rent a scooter in Chiang Mai?

Legally, yes — an International Driving Permit with the motorcycle category, plus your home licence. Many shops rent without checking, but riding uninsured/unlicensed voids travel-insurance medical claims and risks a police fine, so get the IDP before you travel.

Is it safe to ride a scooter in Chiang Mai?

In town, with a helmet and some care, it's manageable and very common. The risks are real though — traffic, mountain roads and the insurance/licence catch above. If you're a nervous rider, use Grab or Bolt instead; the city works fine without a scooter.
