Where to Eat in Chiang Mai: A Local Food Guide

Khao soi, night markets, and the Northern Thai dishes you can't get anywhere else — with the specific places worth queuing for.

Northern Thai food is its own world — earthier, funkier and less sweet than the Bangkok cooking most people know. Chiang Mai is where you eat it at the source. This is what to order and where to go.

Khao soi: the dish you came for

A coconut curry noodle soup topped with crunchy fried noodles, with pickled mustard greens, shallots and lime on the side. It's the signature dish of the north and Chiang Mai does the best in the world. Here are the spots that matter:

WhereAreaWhy go / heads-up
Khao Soi Mae SaiNear Chang PhueakOur top pick — a Michelin Bib Gourmand, superb beef, and (unlike the cult spots) normal hours with no schedule traps.
Khao Soi Khun YaiOld City northBeloved cult spot behind a temple. Cash only, lunch only, and closed part of the week — go early.
Lung Prakit Kad KomNear Kad KomOld-school local favourite. Also cash-only and closes when it sells out.
Khao Soi Lam Duan Fah HamFah Ham (riverside)One of the oldest khao soi houses in the city — a piece of history on the river road.
Khao Soi IslamOld City (Ban Haw)The halal version, in the Muslim quarter by the mosque — richer, with beef and roti.

If you only get one bowl: Khao Soi Mae Sai. If you want the story: pair it with Khun Yai on a morning it's open.

The five meals to build a trip around

Gai yang

SP Chicken

Michelin-listed rotisserie chicken by Wat Phra Singh — smoky, garlicky, unforgettable. Get there before it sells out (often by 10:30–11am).

Northern Thai

Huen Phen

The one-stop Northern spread: sai ua sausage, nam prik ong, khao soi, gaeng hang lay. Casual by day, atmospheric by night.

Khao kha moo

Cowboy Hat Lady

The legendary stewed-pork-leg-over-rice stall (look for the cowboy hat) at Chang Phueak Gate night market. A $2 institution.

Since 1957

Aroon Rai

A canteen-priced old-timer Anthony Bourdain loved — a fine place to try gaeng hang lay (Burmese-influenced pork curry).

Nimman

Tong Tem Toh

The go-to sit-down Northern Thai in the café district — great for a group and a spread of dishes with a beer.

Vegan

Free Bird Café

Chiang Mai is a vegetarian paradise; this social-enterprise café is a lovely, ethical intro to Northern veg cooking.

Northern dishes to seek out

  • Sai ua — the herby Chiang Mai grilled sausage, lemongrass and kaffir lime all through it.
  • Nam prik ong & nam prik noom — a tomato-pork chilli dip and a roasted green-chilli dip, eaten with veg and sticky rice.
  • Gaeng hang lay — rich, gingery Burmese-influenced pork curry, barely spicy, deeply savoury.
  • Khanom jeen nam ngiao — the north's other great noodle soup, tomatoey and tangy, criminally underrated next to khao soi.

✈ Learn to cook it yourself

A half-day cooking class (often with a market tour and a farm visit) is one of the best-value things you can do here — around 1,000–1,500 THB. Thai Farm Cooking School is the original and still the best; Zabb E Lee is the great-value pick.

☼ Eating-well basics

Most street stalls and old-school spots are cash only. The cult places keep short hours and close when they sell out, so eat khao soi at lunch, not dinner. And 'closed on the day you wanted it' is a Chiang Mai rite of passage — always have a backup.

Frequently asked questions

What food is Chiang Mai famous for?

Khao soi — a coconut-curry noodle soup — above all, plus Northern Thai specialities like sai ua (herb sausage), nam prik dips, gaeng hang lay pork curry and khao kha moo (stewed pork leg). The cuisine is distinct from central/Bangkok Thai food.

Where is the best khao soi in Chiang Mai?

Khao Soi Mae Sai is our top pick — a Michelin Bib Gourmand with excellent beef and normal opening hours. Khao Soi Khun Yai and Lung Prakit are beloved cult spots but keep short, cash-only hours.

Is Chiang Mai good for vegetarians and vegans?

Exceptionally — it's one of the best cities in Asia for plant-based food, with places like Free Bird Café, Goodsouls Kitchen and Reform Kafe, plus vegetarian versions of most Northern dishes.
