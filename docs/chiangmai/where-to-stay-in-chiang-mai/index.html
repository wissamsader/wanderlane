Where to Stay in Chiang Mai: Best Areas & Hotels

The four neighborhoods that matter, and the hostels and hotels worth booking in each — from $5 dorms to riverside splurges.

Chiang Mai is small, but where you sleep changes your whole trip. The old town, the café district, the river and the budget quarter each feel like different cities. Here's who each one is for — and exactly where to book.

The four neighborhoods, honestly

AreaBest forThe trade-off
Old CityFirst-timers, temples, walkabilitySome streets are touristy; a few party hostels get noisy
NimmanNomads, cafés, nightlife, shoppingNot walkable to the historic temples; pricier
RiversideCouples, quiet, boutique & luxuryYou'll taxi/scooter to most things; Sept–Oct flood risk
SantithamLong stays, budget, living like a localFew real 'hotels' — mostly guesthouses & co-living

If you only take one thing from this page: first trip → Old City. Here to work → Nimman.

If you're splurging

Chiang Mai's top end is special. 137 Pillars House (a restored teak mansion in the city) and Anantara Chiang Mai (riverside, superb spa) are the in-town icons; the Four Seasons sits among rice terraces out in Mae Rim, 30 minutes north, if you want a resort rather than a city stay.

⚠ Don't book the Dhara Dhevi

The famous palace-like Dhara Dhevi resort is not currently bookable — it's been closed and mid-limbo after fire and ownership issues. If you see it advertised as a live stay, that listing is out of date.

When to book (and the festival trap)

  • High season is November–February. Book two to four weeks ahead, more for boutique places.
  • Yi Peng & Loy Krathong (the lantern festival, mid-to-late November) sells out the whole city months in advance and prices spike. If you're coming for it, book stays before you book anything else.
  • Riverside in September–October: the Ping River has flooded in recent years. Lovely area, just check recent reviews before committing in those months.
  • Green season (June–October) is the cheapest and prettiest for deals — it rains hard but briefly.

Frequently asked questions

Which area of Chiang Mai is best for first-time visitors?

The Old City. It's inside the historic moat, walkable to the major temples and the Sunday Walking Street, and full of cafés and affordable stays. You won't need transport for most of your day.

Is it better to stay in the Old City or Nimman?

Old City for temples, history and walkability; Nimman for cafés, coworking, nightlife and modern comforts. They're a 10-minute drive apart, so many people split their stay.

How much does a hotel in Chiang Mai cost?

Dorms from ~$5, clean private guesthouse rooms from ~$20, characterful boutique hotels $50–130, and genuine luxury $200+. Your money goes remarkably far here compared with most of the world.
