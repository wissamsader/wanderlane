Where to Stay in Beirut: Best Areas & Hotels

Five neighborhoods with genuinely different personalities, and the real hotels worth booking in each — from a budget stay in Hamra to an Ottoman-era mansion in Achrafieh.

Updated July 20268 min read
Home/Beirut/Where to Stay in Beirut: Best Areas & Hotels

Beirut doesn't have one obvious place to sleep — it has five neighborhoods with genuinely different personalities, and where you land shapes the whole trip. Here's an honest breakdown of each, and the specific hotels worth booking, from a straightforward budget stay in Hamra to an Ottoman-era mansion up in Achrafieh.

The five neighborhoods, honestly

NeighborhoodBest forThe trade-off
HamraFirst-timers who want to walk to everything — AUB, cafés, bookshops, and a big share of Beirut's best foodBusy and a little frayed at the edges most hours of the day; don't expect quiet
Mar MikhaelBeing right inside Beirut's best nightlife stripLoud after dark, and none of the hotels we could confirm as live and bookable sit right on the strip itself
GemmayzehCharm — French-mandate buildings, street art, bars a stair-climb from Mar MikhaelSame story as Mar Mikhael: check what's actually bookable here before you count on it
AchrafiehA quieter, elegant hillside base with the city's best boutique hotelsFurther from the Hamra food scene; you'll taxi or walk more to reach it
BadaroA bohemian, tree-lined base by the National Museum and Horsh Beirut parkFewer bars and late-night options right on your doorstep

If you only take one thing from this page: first trip → Hamra. Want quiet and boutique → Achrafieh. (Downtown doesn't get its own row here, but two of the hotels below — the InterContinental and Saifi Suites — are there if you'd rather be near the Souks.)

☼ Getting between neighborhoods

Each of these is walkable in itself, but the sidewalks between them are patchy and distances read shorter on a map than they feel on foot. A short Bolt or taxi hop — Hamra to Mar Mikhael, Achrafieh to Gemmayzeh — is normal, cheap, and how most people actually get around.

Real hotels worth booking

Beirut's hotel scene took a real hit after 2020 and is still rebuilding — but a handful of properties are well-run, currently bookable, and worth reconfirming close to your travel dates. These four span budget to heritage-luxury, spread across four different neighborhoods.

⚠ Before you book

Lebanon carries formal government travel advisories that vary by country and change often — this guide doesn't make an independent safety call. Check your own government's current, dated advisory before you book anything, and again closer to your trip: travel.state.gov for US citizens, gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice for UK citizens.

☼ When to book

Summer (July–August) is Beirut's busiest, priciest season, as the diaspora comes home — book boutique properties like Albergo or Smallville several weeks out if you're traveling then. Outside summer, a week or two ahead is normally plenty.

Frequently asked questions

Which neighborhood is best for a first trip to Beirut?

Hamra. It's walkable, central, dense with the food scene from our eat guide, and a short, cheap taxi hop from the Mar Mikhael and Gemmayzeh nightlife strips if you want to go out at night without sleeping in the middle of it.

How much does a hotel in Beirut cost?

From a simple budget stay in Hamra, up through boutique properties around $200 (The Smallville Hotel) to heritage-luxury addresses like Hotel Albergo or the InterContinental Phoenicia at £220–380. Bring USD cash regardless of where you stay — Beirut still runs largely on cash.

Should I book a refundable rate for a Beirut hotel?

It's a reasonable habit here specifically because the situation is fluid and hotel supply is still rebuilding post-2020 — a flexible or refundable rate costs little extra and removes one thing to worry about. Check your government's current advisory before locking in any non-refundable booking.
